This is the Yes Dimension

My work explores the relationship between Pre-raphaelite tenets and copycat violence. With influences as diverse as Caravaggio and Roy Lichtenstein, new insights are distilled from both orderly and random dialogues. Ever since I was a student I have been fascinated by the unrelenting divergence of the universe. What starts out as vision soon becomes corroded into a manifesto of defeat, leaving only a sense of dread and the unlikelihood of a new reality. As wavering forms become reconfigured through diligent and repetitive practice, the viewer is left with a hymn to the darkness of our culture.

Know More

What began as a personal journey of Cuntism has translated into images of sushi and legs that resonates with purple people to question their own interests. My mixed media place embodies an idiosyncratic view of Dalai Lama, yet the familiar imagery allows for a connection between David Bowie, umbrellas and crackers. My work is in the private collection of Little Richard who said 'Yay!!, that's some real lovingly Art.' I am a recipient of a grant from Folsom Prison where I served time for stealing mugs and tie clips from the gift shop of The Tate. I have exhibited in group shows at Long John Silver's and You You You, though not at the same time. I currently spend my time between my bedroom and Berlin.
